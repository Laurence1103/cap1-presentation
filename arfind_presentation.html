AR-Based Office Finder
with Multiple Route Navigation
for Partido State University

ARFind is a mobile app that shows students and visitors where to go on campus — using augmented reality arrows on their phone camera, in real time. No more getting lost. No more asking for directions.

Problem Setting

Why ARFind Exists

ARFind was created to solve a real and daily problem on campus. This is the story of two people: a student who got lost, and an office staff member who keeps getting interrupted. Both are dealing with the same missing system.

"
🎒
Juan dela Cruz, 18
First-year student · New Enrollee · Partido State University

It was Juan's first day at Partido State University. He needed to go to the Registrar's Office to submit his enrollment documents. Nobody told him where it was, and the campus has several buildings spread across the grounds.

He asked a guard at the gate, who pointed him toward the main building. He walked there — but it was the wrong building. He asked a student passing by, who told him to go to the second floor of another building. He went there. That was the Cashier, not the Registrar. He walked back down and asked again.

By the time he found the right office, it was already 11:30 AM. He had been walking and asking for directions for almost an hour. He almost missed the submission window because of it.

"Wala talaga akong alam kung saan yung opisina. Nakakahiya pa na paulit-ulit kang nagtatanong."

There was no map, no digital guide, and no system to help him find the office on his own. That is the problem ARFind is solving.

"
💼
Ate Maria Santos, 34
Office Staff · Records Section · Partido State University

Ate Maria works at the Records Section. During enrollment period, she handles important documents — but she cannot finish her work without being interrupted. Every few minutes, someone opens the door and asks: "Ma'am, nasaan po ang Cashier?" or "Kuya, pwede ba dito mag-enroll?"

On some days, she is asked for directions more than 20 times before lunch. Each time, she has to stop what she is doing to explain where the office is. The same question. The same answer. Over and over.

The problem is that there is no system to guide visitors on their own. No digital map. No signage that is easy to follow. No app. So they walk into the nearest open door and ask whoever is inside.

"Minsan kailangan ko pang lumabas para ituro personally kasi hindi maintindihan sa salita," she said. "Masyadong madalas. Hindi na ako nakakatapos ng trabaho."

She does not mind helping. But she should not have to stop working 20 times a day just to give directions. ARFind can do that for her.

Why This Matters

This is not just getting lost. It wastes time for everyone.

Students and visitors lose time walking to wrong buildings and asking multiple people for directions. Office staff lose focus because they are constantly interrupted by navigation questions. And during enrollment — when the campus is at its busiest — this problem gets worse every day. There is no digital tool at Partido State University that helps people find their way on their own. ARFind was built to fix that.

Project Concept

What ARFind Actually Does

ARFind is not just a map. It is a two-platform system: a mobile AR navigation app for students and visitors, and a web admin dashboard for campus staff to manage all office information and routes.

Proposed Solution
Mobile Application
For Students and Visitors

Built with React Native to run on both Android and iOS. Simple enough for a first-year student who has never been to the campus before — or a visitor who only comes once.

  • ✓ Search for any office or department by name
  • ✓ See AR arrows and office markers on your camera view in real time
  • ✓ Choose from multiple routes — shortest path or accessible path
  • ✓ View office details: personnel assigned, services offered, and schedule
  • ✓ Works both outdoors (GPS) and indoors (AR anchors)
Web Admin Dashboard
For Campus Administrators

A browser-based management panel built with React.js. Works on any desktop or laptop with no installation needed. Campus staff use it to keep office information and routes current.

  • ✓ Add, update, or remove office locations and information
  • ✓ Manage personnel names and services offered per office
  • ✓ Update route paths and AR anchor points on the campus map
  • ✓ Set office schedules and transaction windows
  • ✓ Keep the campus directory current for all users
AR Navigation

Directional arrows and office markers appear on the user's camera view. They just point their phone and follow the guide — no map-reading needed.

Office Search

Search any office or department by name. The app finds it and starts the AR guide right away — no need to know the building or floor number.

Multiple Route Options

Users choose from more than one route to the same office — a shorter path or a more accessible one for people with mobility concerns.

Office Information Display

Each office marker shows the personnel assigned, services offered, and schedule. Users know what to expect before they even arrive.

Web Admin Dashboard

Campus administrators update office locations, personnel details, and navigation routes through a simple web dashboard accessible on any desktop browser.

Built for PSU Goa

ARFind is made specifically for Partido State University in Goa, Camarines Sur — designed to fit the actual campus layout and how the university operates.

Scope and Boundaries

What's Included and What's Not

Being clear about scope keeps the system focused and deliverable within the project timeline.

✅ In Scope
  • ✓ Mobile app for Android and iOS
  • ✓ AR navigation with directional arrows and markers on camera
  • ✓ Office search by name or department
  • ✓ Multiple route options per destination
  • ✓ Office information display — personnel, services, schedule
  • ✓ Web admin dashboard for campus staff
  • ✓ GPS for outdoor areas + AR anchors for indoor spaces
❌ Out of Scope
  • ✗ Appointment booking or online queuing
  • ✗ Student portal or grade viewing
  • ✗ Multi-campus support (PSU Goa only)
  • ✗ Integration with government or university systems
  • ✗ AI chatbot or automated responses
  • ✗ Other university campuses or private buildings
User Flow

From Phone to Office in 4 Steps

Simple enough for a first-year student who has never been to campus before, or a visitor who only comes once.

1
Search

Open the ARFind app. Type the name of the office you need — like "Registrar" or "Cashier." The app finds it right away.

2
Choose Route

Pick from the available routes. The app shows options — shortest path or accessible path. You choose what works best for you.

3
Follow AR

Point your camera and walk. Arrows and markers appear on your screen showing where to go. Just follow what you see on the camera.

4
Arrive

The app tells you when you have arrived. You can also read the office information — personnel and services — so you know what to do next.

Admin Flow

How Campus Staff Manages the System

Open the Dashboard

Admin opens the web admin on any desktop browser. No installation needed. All office information and routes are visible and ready to manage.

Update Office Info

Add or edit office names, personnel, services, and schedules. Changes appear immediately on the mobile app for all users on campus.

Manage Route Paths

Update navigation routes and AR anchor points as needed — such as when a building is closed or a new route becomes available.

Keep It Updated

During enrollment or busy periods, admins can quickly update the system so students always get the right directions to the right offices.

Technical Background

The Stack Behind ARFind

Built on tools that are industry-standard, open-source, and well-documented. The right choice for a student capstone team building a real AR navigation system.

Mobile Frontend
React Native
Android and iOS
ARCore (Android)
ARKit (iOS)

React Native allows a single codebase to run on both Android and iOS. ARCore and ARKit handle the augmented reality — detecting the physical space and placing arrows on the camera view.

Web Admin Frontend
React.js
Responsive UI
Chrome / Edge
Campus Map Manager

The admin dashboard runs in any modern browser — no installation needed for campus staff. Built to be easy to use on a standard desktop monitor for managing offices and routes.

Backend and Database
Node.js + Express
MySQL / PostgreSQL
REST API
AR Anchor Storage

Node.js + Express handles all system logic — office data, route calculation, AR anchor storage, and user queries. The database stores all office records, personnel, routes, and campus map data.

Positioning and DevTools
GPS (outdoor)
AR Anchors (indoor)
Git and GitHub
Visual Studio Code

GPS handles outdoor areas of the campus. Image-based AR anchors handle indoor spaces where GPS signals are unreliable — so navigation works both inside and outside buildings.

Hardware Requirements

Devices and equipment needed to develop and use ARFind, for the team and for end users.

Empathize · Define · Ideate

Design Thinking Process

We used the Design Thinking framework to make sure the solution was based on real user problems — not just assumptions about what a campus navigation system should do.

Empathize

We looked at the experience of students like Juan — new enrollees who waste time walking to wrong buildings — and staff like Ate Maria who are interrupted by direction questions all day. The frustration was real on both sides.

Define

The core problem: there is no digital system at Partido State University that lets students and visitors find offices on their own. No app, no digital map, no real-time guide — only asking people for directions or reading old printed signages.

Ideate

We looked at different options: printed maps, SMS guides, and QR code signs. We decided that a mobile AR navigation app with a web admin was the most practical and modern solution for a state university setting like PSU.
