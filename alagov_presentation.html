Problem Setting

Why AlaGov Exists

AlaGov was created to solve a real and daily problem at Goa Municipal Hall. This is the story of two people: a resident who wasted a whole morning, and a government staff member who answers the same questions every day. Both are stuck in the same broken system.

"
🧓
Nanay Rosa Maglente, 61
Resident · Barangay Taytay · Goa, Camarines Sur

Nanay Rosa needed a barangay clearance to apply for a senior citizen ID. She woke up early, asked her grandson to bring her to the barangay hall by tricycle, and waited in line for 40 minutes.

When she finally reached the counter, the staff told her — "Ma'am, kailangan mo muna ng valid ID at proof of residency bago namin maproseso ito." She did not bring any of those. Nobody told her what to bring beforehand. There was no list to check, no number to call, no app to ask.

She went home and came back two days later with her documents. That was her second trip for one transaction. Her grandson took half a day off work both times.

"Kung may mapagtanungan lang sana ako bago pa man pumunta. Matanda na ako, nahihirapan akong pumunta ng paulit-ulit."

There was no way for her to check the requirements before going. That is the problem AlaGov is solving.

"
🏛️
Kuya Arnel Bautista, 38
Frontline Staff · Goa Municipal Hall · Information Window

Kuya Arnel works at the information window of Goa Municipal Hall. Every morning, before he can even start processing documents, he spends the first two hours answering the same questions over and over.

"Ano ang requirements ng business permit?" — 12 times a day. "Magkano ang bayad sa birth certificate?" — 8 times. "Nasaan ang Civil Registry office?" — at least 15. He estimates that he spends 30 to 50 percent of his working day just answering basic questions that could be answered digitally.

During peak periods — like enrollment season or barangay election time — the queue at his window stretches outside the building. People wait 30 to 45 minutes just to ask a simple question. There is no system to help them find the answer on their own.

"Gusto kong makatulong sa mas malalim na transaksyon. Pero halos kalahati ng araw ko, basic questions lang ang sinasagot ko," he said. "Walang sistema na sumasagot para sa akin."

He does not want to stop helping. But a chatbot that answers the simple questions frees him to handle the ones that actually need a human. That is what AlaGov is built to do.

Why This Matters

This is not just inconvenient. It is a barrier to basic government access.

For elderly residents like Nanay Rosa, every wasted trip costs time, money, and physical effort. For low-income families, a half-day of work lost to a failed transaction has real consequences. And for staff like Kuya Arnel, spending half the day on repetitive questions means less time for the transactions that actually need human attention. AlaGov was built to remove that barrier — by giving residents the information they need, in the language they speak, before they even leave their home.

Project Concept

What AlaGov Actually Does

AlaGov is not a full e-government portal. It is a focused two-platform system: a Bikol/Filipino NLP chatbot for residents, and an analytics admin dashboard for LGU staff. It solves one specific problem at one specific place.

Proposed Solution
Mobile and Web Chatbot
For Residents

Accessible on any smartphone browser or as a mobile app. Residents type their question in Bikol or Filipino — just like texting — and get a real, accurate answer right away.

  • ✓ Ask questions about LGU services in Bikol or Filipino natural language
  • ✓ Get the full list of requirements, fees, and steps for any service
  • ✓ Book an appointment via chat — chatbot routes to the correct office
  • ✓ Get escalated to a human staff member if the chatbot cannot answer
  • ✓ Works on any smartphone — no app download required
Web Admin Dashboard
For LGU Staff

A browser-based admin panel for Goa Municipal Hall and barangay offices. Staff keep the service database updated and use the analytics dashboard to see what residents are asking about most.

  • ✓ Manage the service requirement database — requirements, fees, steps per service
  • ✓ Monitor real-time inquiry volumes and most-asked questions
  • ✓ Review and respond to escalated queries flagged by the chatbot
  • ✓ View a heatmap of unresolved queries to improve service delivery
  • ✓ See peak inquiry times and plan staff allocation accordingly
Bikol / Filipino NLP Chatbot

Residents type in Bikol or Filipino — the chatbot understands their intent and gives the right answer. No need to use English or navigate a website menu.

Intent Classification

The NLP layer identifies what the resident is asking about — permit, certificate, schedule, fee — and maps it to the correct service in the database automatically.

Appointment Booking via Chat

Residents book appointments directly through the chatbot. The system identifies which office to route them to and confirms the schedule — no phone call needed.

Human Escalation

When the chatbot cannot answer a complex query, it flags the inquiry and routes it to a human staff member — with full context already attached so the staff can respond quickly.

Analytics Information System

The admin dashboard shows inquiry volumes, most-asked questions, unresolved query heatmaps, and peak times — so LGU staff can improve service delivery based on real data.

Built for Goa, CamSur

AlaGov is made specifically for Goa Municipal Hall and barangay offices in Goa, Camarines Sur — using the Bikol language and local government service structure that residents actually use.

Scope and Boundaries

What's Included and What's Not

Being clear about scope keeps the system focused and deliverable.

✅ In Scope
  • ✓ Bikol/Filipino NLP chatbot (mobile and web)
  • ✓ Intent classification for LGU service queries
  • ✓ Service requirement database (admin-updatable)
  • ✓ Appointment booking via chat
  • ✓ Human escalation for unresolved queries
  • ✓ Analytics dashboard for LGU staff
  • ✓ Goa Municipal Hall and barangay offices only
❌ Out of Scope
  • ✗ Online payment or cashless transactions
  • ✗ Full e-government portal features
  • ✗ Document processing or digital filing
  • ✗ Integration with national government systems
  • ✗ Multi-municipality support
  • ✗ Real-time queue management
Resident Flow

From Question to Answer in 4 Steps

Designed to work for a 61-year-old lola typing in Bikol on her grandson's phone, or a working adult asking on their lunch break.

1
Ask

Open AlaGov on any browser or the app. Type your question in Bikol or Filipino — like texting. No forms, no menus.

2
Understand

The NLP layer reads the question, identifies what you need — permit, certificate, schedule, fee — and looks it up in the service database.

3
Get Answer

The chatbot replies with the full requirements, fees, steps, and the office to go to. You can also book an appointment directly through the chat.

4
Go Ready

You arrive at the hall with everything prepared. One trip. No missing documents. No wasted time waiting in line just to ask a question.

Staff Flow

How LGU Staff Uses the System

Morning — Dashboard Review

Staff opens the admin dashboard and sees overnight inquiry volume, the most-asked questions from the previous day, and any escalations flagged by the chatbot that need a human reply.

During the Day — Update Services

When requirements or fees change, staff updates the service database directly in the admin panel. The chatbot reflects the new information immediately for all residents.

Respond to Escalations

When the chatbot flags a complex query it cannot answer, staff sees the full conversation context and responds directly. The resident gets a human reply without having to come to the hall.

Review Analytics

The heatmap shows which services generate the most unresolved queries. Staff uses this data to improve the database, add missing information, and plan for busy periods.

Empathize · Define · Ideate

Design Thinking Process

We used the Design Thinking framework to make sure AlaGov was built around real resident and staff needs, not just assumptions about what a government chatbot should do.

Empathize

We looked at the experience of residents like Nanay Rosa, elderly and with low digital literacy, who make wasted trips because they had no way to check requirements. And staff like Kuya Arnel who answer the same basic questions all day instead of handling real transactions.

Define

The core problem: there is no digital system at Goa Municipal Hall that lets residents check LGU service requirements, fees, and schedules in their own language before going. No Bikol chatbot exists for any LGU in Camarines Sur.

Ideate

We looked at different options: Facebook messenger bots, SMS systems, and printed QR codes. We concluded that a two-platform NLP chatbot with an admin analytics dashboard was the most practical and scalable solution for a rural LGU setting.
