Problem Setting

Why GenRx Exists

GenRx was created to solve a real problem. Not just numbers from a study, but actual situations that happen every day. This is the story of two people: a patient and a pharmacy staff. Both are dealing with the same problem, just from different sides. Research confirms that many small-scale pharmacies still depend on manual processes, frequently face stock shortages, and have long service times. (Soliveres, Herrera, & Cedillo, 2024)

"
Aling Nena Santos, 67
Lola of 4 · Maintenance patient · Taytay, Goa, Camarines Sur

Every Monday morning, Aling Nena wakes up at 6 AM and walks 35 minutes to The Generics Pharmacy in Goa. She does not have a smartphone. She buys the same two medicines every week: Amlodipine 5mg for her blood pressure and Metformin 500mg for her diabetes. She has been doing this for three years.

Two Mondays ago, she arrived at 7:40 AM and waited in line for 40 minutes. When she reached the counter, the pharmacist told her — "Ma'am, wala na po kaming Metformin ngayon. Baka bukas o makalawa pa."

She walked back home with nothing. Her son Rodel took half a day off work on Wednesday to bring her by tricycle. The medicine was still out of stock. They came back on Friday. That was the third trip in one week, and three days without her complete medication.

"Kung may paraan lang sana para malaman ko bago pa man pumunta. Para hindi na ako magsasayang ng lakad."

There was no way for her to check if the medicine was available before making the trip. That is the problem GenRx is solving.

"
Kuya Mark Reyes, 29
Pharmacy Staff · The Generics Pharmacy, Goa · 4 years on the counter

Kuya Mark starts his shift at 7 AM. Before opening the pharmacy, he manually counts every medicine on the shelf using a ballpen and a printed stock list. This takes him 45 minutes. By 7:45 AM, customers are already lining up outside.

Last month, he ran out of Amlodipine 10mg on a Tuesday. He only found out when a patient asked for it at the counter. The shelf was empty. There were only two tablets left in the back room. He had to turn away five patients that day, two of them had walked more than a kilometer to get there.

There was no warning. No alert. No system to tell him the stock was running low. Just a manual list and the hope he would notice before it ran out. When many patients come at the same time, like after a health mission or on a payday weekend, the stock runs out faster than he can track by hand.

"The hard part is you never know it's running out until it's already gone," he said. "There's no warning. No system to tell you in advance."

He only needs one thing: a way to know when a medicine is running low before it runs out, not after.

Why This Matters

This is not just inconvenience. It is a health risk.

Amlodipine and Metformin are maintenance medicines. Patients need to take them every day to manage their condition. If they miss even a few days, it can lead to serious health problems, especially for elderly patients with diabetes or high blood pressure. In Goa, The Generics Pharmacy is often the only affordable place where patients can buy these medicines. When it runs out of stock, patients like Aling Nena have no other option. GenRx was built to prevent that from happening.

Related Studies

What Research Says

GenRx builds on existing studies that validate each of its core components — QR-based medicine verification, digital inventory management, pharmacy digitization in the Philippines, and mobile app design for rural communities.

QR Code Verification
Ti, Chen, & Wu (2020)

Developed a QR code-based system for medication administration and showed that QR-based verification can reduce errors and speed up medicine release at the counter. GenRx uses this same approach — generating a unique QR code per order that pharmacy staff scan to confirm and release the customer's medicines.

Inventory Management
Soliveres, Herrera, & Cedillo (2024)

Studied inventory management in small-scale pharmacies in Cavite and found that most still use manual record-keeping, which leads to stock inaccuracies and unexpected shortages. This directly supports the need for GenRx's real-time inventory monitoring and low-stock alert features in similar local pharmacy settings like those in Camarines Sur.

Pharmacy Digitization (PH)
Dorado et al. (2023)

Built a web-based pharmacy management system for a local drugstore in the Philippines that included online ordering and inventory tracking. Their study showed that going digital reduced waiting time and improved customer satisfaction — exactly what GenRx aims to achieve through its two-platform approach.

Mobile Apps in Rural Communities
Rathod & Patel (2021)

Found that simple and lightweight mobile apps are more likely to be used in rural communities with limited internet access. This guided the design of the GenRx mobile application to be clean and easy to use even on basic smartphones with limited data.

Project Concept

What GenRx Actually Does

GenRx is not a delivery app. It's not a health marketplace. It's a focused, practical two-platform system that addresses one specific problem at one specific pharmacy and does it well.

Proposed Solution
Mobile Application
For Customers

Built with React Native to run on both Android and iOS. Designed to be simple enough for elderly patients to use, or for a family member to use on their behalf.

  • ✓ Browse the full medicine catalog with real-time stock status before leaving home
  • ✓ Place an order and reserve the medicine remotely with no need to wait in line
  • ✓ Receive a unique QR code per order, saved directly on your phone
  • ✓ Upload a photo of your prescription in-app for regulated medicines
  • ✓ Get a real-time notification when your order is ready for pickup
  • ✓ Show your QR code at the counter — no form, no manual request, no queue
Web Admin Dashboard
For Pharmacy Staff

A website that staff can open on any browser, built with React.js. Works on any desktop or laptop with no installation needed. Designed to replace the manual logbook with real information on what's in stock.

  • ✓ View real-time inventory levels for every medicine, updated automatically
  • ✓ Receive automatic low-stock alerts before a medicine actually runs out
  • ✓ See the incoming order queue and manage each order's status
  • ✓ Scan customer QR codes at the counter to verify and release orders instantly
  • ✓ View prescription photo uploads and approve before releasing regulated medicines
  • ✓ Track order history and stock movements over time
Live Inventory Sync

Every medicine's stock count is reflected in real-time on both platforms. What a customer sees in the app matches exactly what's on the shelf at that moment.

Early Low-Stock Alerts

Staff receive automatic alerts when a medicine's stock falls below a set threshold, giving them days, not hours, to reorder before it runs out. (Soliveres, Herrera, & Cedillo, 2024)

QR Code Verification

Each confirmed order generates a unique, server-stored QR code. Staff scan it at the counter to immediately retrieve and verify the order. No manual lookup, no confusion. (Ti, Chen, & Wu, 2020)

Prescription Upload and Review

Regulated medicines requiring a prescription are handled digitally. Customers upload a photo, staff review it, and can approve or request clarification before the order is released.

Counter Pickup Only

GenRx does not involve delivery operations. It's designed specifically for QR-based counter pickup, a practical model for a community pharmacy where home delivery isn't possible.

Built for Goa, CamSur

This is not a general solution. GenRx is scoped specifically for the Generic Pharmacy branch in Goa, Camarines Sur, to make sure it actually fits how that branch operates. Digitizing pharmacy systems like this has been shown to reduce waiting time and improve customer satisfaction. (Dorado et al., 2023)

  • ✓ Order placement and QR code generation

  • ✗ Third-party pharmacy integration
Proposed Solution

From Phone to Counter in 4 Steps

Designed to work for a 67-year-old lola, a working parent ordering on behalf of a family member, and everyone in between.

1
Browse

Open the GenRx app. Search for the medicine by name or browse the catalog. The stock status shows in real-time. Green means available now.

2
Order

Tap to order. If the medicine requires a prescription, upload a photo before confirming. The order goes to the pharmacy queue instantly.

3
Get QR

A unique QR code is generated and saved in the app. You receive a push notification the moment the pharmacy staff marks your order ready.

4
Pick Up

Go to the pharmacy counter. Show your QR code. Staff scans it to verify your order and hands over your medicine. No waiting in line. Done.

Order Verification

Unique per Order. Verified at the Counter.

Each order generates a unique QR code stored in the backend. When scanned by pharmacy staff, it instantly retrieves the order details: patient name, medicines, prescription status, and marks it as released. No manual lookup, no errors, no fake orders.

Proposed Solution

How Staff Manages the Day

Morning — Dashboard Check

Staff opens the web admin on the counter PC. Dashboard shows current stock levels, overnight orders, and any low-stock alerts from the previous day, all before the pharmacy opens.

During the Day — Order Queue

Incoming orders appear in real-time on the order queue. Staff prepares each order, marks it as "Ready," which triggers the customer's pickup notification automatically.

At the Counter — QR Scan

When a customer arrives, staff scans their QR code using the dashboard's scanner interface. The order details appear immediately. One click to release and mark complete.

Anytime — Low-Stock Alerts

When a medicine's stock falls below the configured minimum, the dashboard flags it. Staff sees the alert immediately, in time to act before the next patient who needs it arrives.

Empathize · Define · Ideate

Design Thinking Process

Before building GenRx, we first took time to understand the real problems of the people who would use it: the patients and the pharmacy staff.

Empathize

We looked at the situation of patients like Aling Nena, elderly people who travel to the pharmacy only to find their medicine is out of stock. And staff like Kuya Mark who count inventory by hand every morning with no system to help them.

Define

The main problem is clear: there is no digital tool that lets customers check if medicine is available before going to the pharmacy, and no system that alerts staff when a medicine is about to run out. Both sides are suffering from the same gap. No technology to help them. Manual record-keeping in small pharmacies directly leads to stock inaccuracies and unexpected shortages. (Soliveres, Herrera, & Cedillo, 2024)

Ideate

We considered different solutions: home delivery, SMS alerts, walk-in improvements, and decided that a mobile app for customers and a web dashboard for staff, with QR code pickup, is the most practical and affordable option for a small pharmacy in Goa. A QR-based verification approach has been proven to reduce errors and speed up medicine release. (Ti, Chen, & Wu, 2020)
